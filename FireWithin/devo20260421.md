As Jesus approached the final hours before the cross, He turned His
attention to His disciples in a deeply personal way. What He chose to
do was not merely symbolic - it was instructive. Though He is Lord
over all, He took the position of a servant and washed the feet of
those who followed Him. In doing so, He revealed both the nature of
His kingdom and the character of His heart.

This act was not about establishing a ritual, but about setting a
pattern. The One who possesses all authority willingly lowered Himself
to serve others. This reminds us that true greatness in God’s eyes is
not measured by status, but by humility. Jesus did not serve because
His disciples deserved it, but because it reflected who He is -
gracious, patient, and self-giving.

Even more striking is that He served all of them, including the one
who would betray Him. This shows that Christian service is not based
on the worthiness of others, but on obedience to Christ. When we serve
in this way, we guard our hearts from pride, resentment, and
bitterness, and instead reflect the mercy we ourselves have received.

There is also a call to humility in receiving. Peter initially
resisted, not understanding that to refuse Christ’s service was to
miss fellowship with Him. In the same way, we must allow both Christ
and others to minister to us, recognizing our dependence and setting
aside pride.

Jesus concludes with a promise: there is blessing in obedience. Not
simply in knowing His example, but in following it. As we walk in
humble service, we align our lives with His, and in that place, we
experience the quiet blessing of God.
