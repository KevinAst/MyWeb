Scripture teaches that God has not left Himself without witness in the
world (M{ bibleLink(`act.14.17@@Acts 14:17`) }M). 
From the beginning, He has made His presence known through what
He has created. The order, beauty, and complexity of creation testify
to His power and divine nature. This is not a saving revelation, but
it is a true and sufficient witness that points to the reality of God.

In addition to creation, God has given every person a conscience
(M{ bibleLink(`rom.2.14-15@@Romans 2:14–15`) }M).
This inward awareness reflects a moral standard beyond ourselves. Even
without written law, people recognize right and wrong, which points to
a Lawgiver who is righteous and just. In this way, God reveals both
His existence and His holiness.

Yet Scripture also makes clear that while creation and conscience
reveal God, they do not fully reveal the way of salvation. The gospel
of Jesus Christ is the clear message through which people come to know
God personally and receive forgiveness of sins. Therefore, the
testimony of creation and conscience serves to make humanity
accountable and to stir the heart to seek Him.

God, in His sovereignty, is able to reach any person in any place. He
is not limited in how He makes Himself known. While we may not
understand all the ways He works, we can trust that He is just,
merciful, and faithful to reveal Himself according to His perfect
will.

This truth calls us to two responses: to trust God’s justice in how He
reveals Himself to others, and to be faithful in sharing the gospel
where we have opportunity. What God has made known, we are responsible
to receive—and to proclaim.
