God has always made a way for His people to come into His presence,
but He has never left that approach undefined. From the earliest days
of Israel’s worship, the Lord established clear patterns to teach His
people both His holiness and His grace. The temple, with all its
careful design and structure, was not merely architectural detail—it
was a visible reminder that God Himself is the One who establishes and
strengthens those who draw near to Him.

The place where the temple was built carried a history marked by both
failure and mercy. What began as a site connected to human sin became,
by God’s provision, a place of worship and restoration. This reveals
something important about the character of God: He redeems what is
broken and uses it for His purposes. He does not ignore sin, but He
makes a way for reconciliation.

Every element of worship pointed to a deeper truth - that access to
God is not casual, nor is it earned by human effort. It is granted by
His design and fulfilled ultimately through Christ. What once required
priests, sacrifices, and separation has now been opened through Jesus,
who made a way for us to come directly before God.

Yet the principle remains the same: when we come before the Lord, we
do so with reverence, humility, and dependence. We are not entering
merely a place, but acknowledging His presence. And in that posture,
God does what only He can do - He establishes our lives and
strengthens our hearts.

The Lord is still faithful to meet those who come to Him rightly. He
forms what concerns us, not according to our strength, but according
to His enduring mercy.
