The question of whether believers will witness the rise of the
Antichrist leads us to consider not speculation, but what Scripture
clearly reveals about God’s care for His people. In Revelation 3:10,
Jesus speaks to a faithful church and gives a promise: those who have
kept His word will be kept from a coming global hour of trial. This is
not merely protection within the trial, but a keeping from it.

Throughout Scripture, we see a consistent pattern of God
distinguishing between the righteous and the wicked in times of
judgment. His character is both just and merciful. He does not pour
out His wrath indiscriminately, but makes a way for His people to be
spared. This reflects His covenant faithfulness and His intimate
knowledge of those who belong to Him.

For the believer, this truth is not meant to stir fear or curiosity
about future events, but to produce steady confidence in Christ. Our
security is not in our understanding of timelines, but in our
relationship with the Lord who holds all things in His hands. The
emphasis of Scripture is not on identifying the Antichrist, but on
remaining faithful to Jesus.

Therefore, the call for us today is clear: persevere in obedience,
remain rooted in truth, and walk closely with Christ. God’s promises
are not given to satisfy our questions about the future, but to anchor
our hearts in His faithfulness. Whatever unfolds in the world, the
Lord knows how to keep His people.
