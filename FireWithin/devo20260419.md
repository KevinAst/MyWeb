In M{ bibleLink(`ecc.5@@Ecclesiastes 5`) }M, Solomon turns from
observing the burdens of life to addressing how we approach God. He
reminds us that worship is not merely an outward activity but a matter
of the heart. To “guard your steps” is to come before God with careful
attention, recognizing His holiness and our position before Him.

The emphasis is not on external forms, but on inward reverence. God is
not honored by empty words or careless expressions of devotion.
Instead, He calls His people to draw near with a listening
posture. This kind of listening is not passive; it is attentive and
responsive. It reflects a heart that is ready to obey.

Solomon warns against what he calls the “sacrifice of fools”—words
spoken without thought, promises made without intention to follow
through. Such worship lacks sincerity and disregards the weight of
God’s presence. The Lord is in heaven, sovereign and holy, and we are
on earth, dependent and finite. This truth should shape the way we
approach Him.

True worship, then, is marked by reverence, humility, and
integrity. It is not confined to a moment or a place but flows from a
life that seeks to honor God consistently. When we come before Him,
whether privately or among others, we are to come thoughtfully, ready
to confess, to give thanks, and to hear His Word.

This passage calls us to examine our approach to God. Are we hurried,
distracted, or careless? Or do we come with a heart that recognizes
His worth and responds accordingly? Worship that pleases God begins
with a right view of Him and a sincere response from us.
