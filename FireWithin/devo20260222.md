The opening chapter of M{ bibleLink(`1sa.1@@1 Samuel`) }M introduces
us to a painful season in the life of Hannah. She was loved by her
husband, yet burdened by barrenness. In a culture where children
were seen as a sign of blessing, her empty arms became a source of
deep sorrow. Scripture tells us plainly that the Lord had closed her
womb. This was not random. God is the author of life, and nothing in
Hannah’s story was outside His sovereign hand.

What makes this account remarkable is not merely her anguish, but
where she carried it. She went to the house of the Lord. Year after
year, through provocation and tears, she placed herself in the
presence of God. And there, in bitterness of soul, she prayed.

Notice how she addresses Him: “O Lord of hosts.” This is a title of
strength and authority—the Commander of heavenly armies. Yet the God
who rules over angelic hosts also bends low to hear one grieving
woman. Hannah’s prayer reveals a heart surrendered to God’s
purposes. She did not simply ask for relief; she promised that if God
granted her a son, she would dedicate him back to the Lord.

In time, God would answer. But even before the answer came, her
countenance changed. Peace replaced despair. Why? Because she
entrusted her pain to the Lord who hears.

There are seasons when God’s purposes are hidden from us. We may not
understand why He allows certain hardships. But Hannah’s story reminds
us that the Lord of hosts is also the Lord who listens. He works not
only through our blessings, but through our waiting.

Bring your sorrow honestly before Him. Trust His timing. The God who
governs nations also governs your life with wisdom and care.
