The desire for marriage is good and God-given, yet Scripture teaches
that even this desire must be placed under a greater priority: seeking
the kingdom of God first. When Jesus speaks these words, He is not
dismissing human needs but properly ordering them. A relationship,
including marriage, is not meant to be the foundation of life, but a
result of a life already grounded in God.

To pursue a spouse rightly begins with pursuing Christ
faithfully. This means placing yourself in environments where God is
honored, His Word is taught, and His people are gathered. Spiritual
direction matters because it shapes both who you are becoming and who
you are likely to meet. Scripture consistently shows that our
companionships influence our walk with God, not merely our
circumstances.

At the same time, trusting God’s sovereignty does not mean
passivity. Faith is not inactivity. Just as God provides daily bread
yet calls us to labor, so also He guides relationships while calling
us to wise and intentional steps. There is a responsibility to walk in
obedience, to grow in spiritual maturity, and to seek relationships
that reflect God’s design.

Most importantly, the central quality to look for in a future spouse
is not personality, compatibility, or shared interests, but a genuine
devotion to Jesus Christ. Marriage is sustained not by natural harmony
but by shared submission to the Lord. Where Christ is truly honored,
grace grows, forgiveness is practiced, and unity is strengthened.

When both individuals are seeking Christ first, they are being shaped
by the same truth, led by the same Spirit, and anchored in the same
hope. This becomes the foundation upon which a lasting, God-honoring
marriage is built.
