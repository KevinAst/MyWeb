The Bible speaks plainly about the reality of life after
death. Scripture uses different terms to describe the unseen realm and
the final destiny of souls, but the central truth remains clear: death
is not the end, and judgment is certain.

In the Old Testament, the word Sheol refers to the place of the
dead—where both the righteous and the unrighteous departed. It was
understood as the realm beyond this life. In the New Testament, the
Greek term Hades is used in a similar way, describing the intermediate
state where souls await final resurrection and judgment. Jesus Himself
spoke of a division within this realm in Luke 16, describing comfort
for the faithful and torment for the unrepentant, separated by a great
chasm.

The New Testament also mentions Gehenna, which Jesus used to describe
the final place of judgment—what Revelation calls the lake of
fire. Unlike Hades, which is temporary, Gehenna represents the final
and eternal separation from God. Even Hades itself will one day be
cast into the lake of fire. There is also a reference to Tartarus, a
place of confinement for certain fallen angels, awaiting
judgment. These distinctions are not meant to confuse us but to show
that God has revealed order, justice, and purpose in His plan.

Yet the most important truth is this: because of Christ’s death and
resurrection, the destiny of believers has changed. Those who trust in
Christ no longer await judgment in fear but are promised immediate
presence with the Lord upon death. The grave does not hold the final
word for the redeemed—Jesus does.

Scripture calls us not to speculation, but to readiness. Life is
brief. Eternity is certain. And our response to Christ determines our
destiny.
