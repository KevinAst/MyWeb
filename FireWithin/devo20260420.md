Fear often presses us to act before God has spoken. In moments of
uncertainty, the natural instinct is to take control, to solve the
problem quickly, and to secure our own outcome. Yet Scripture
consistently calls us to a different posture - waiting. Waiting is not
passive resignation; it is active trust in the timing and authority of
God.

When we move ahead of the Lord, even with good intentions, we step
outside of His will. Partial obedience or delayed trust reveals a
deeper struggle within the heart - a reluctance to fully depend on
Him. God is not honored by hurried decisions driven by anxiety, but by
steady Faith in His Word and His promises.

Waiting on the Lord requires humility. It acknowledges that His wisdom
is greater than ours and that His timing is never late. Even when
circumstances seem urgent and pressure increases, God remains
sovereign. He is not constrained by what we see or feel. In fact, He
often allows situations to narrow beyond our ability so that His power
becomes unmistakable.

This kind of trust is not about having perfect faith but about
directing even a small measure of faith toward a faithful God. When we
resist fear and choose to wait, we align ourselves with His
purposes. In that place, God strengthens the heart, clarifies
direction, and accomplishes what we could never achieve on our own.

The Lord is not looking for hurried action but for surrendered
hearts. He is faithful to act in His time and in His way.
