The account of Ananias and Sapphira in M{ bibleLink(`act.5@@Acts 5`) }M
reminds us that God cares deeply about the purity of His church. Their
sin was not that they withheld part of the money from the sale of
their land. Peter made it clear that the property belonged to them and
was under their control. Their sin was deception. They wanted the
appearance of complete devotion while secretly holding back the
truth. In doing so, they lied not only to people, but to the Holy
Spirit Himself.

This passage may seem severe, yet it reveals something important about
the character of God. The early church was being established as a
witness to the world, and God desired His people to understand that
holiness matters. The Lord is gracious and merciful, but He is also
holy and just. Scripture teaches us not to treat sin lightly or to
become comfortable with hypocrisy. God desires truth in the inward
parts of our lives.

The fear of the Lord is not a terrified dread that pushes us away from
God. It is a reverent awareness of who He is. He is our loving Father,
yet He is also the righteous Judge of all the earth. When we live with
that healthy reverence, it shapes our choices, guards our hearts, and
leads us away from deliberate sin.

As believers, we are called to walk honestly before God. We do not
need to impress Him or others with appearances. The Lord already sees
every hidden motive and every private thought. What He desires is
humble obedience, sincere repentance, and hearts that remain tender
before Him through the work of Christ.
